Study Experiment at Chowgule College

Posted on 20th January 2017 at 10:30 am

Parvatibai Chowgule College of Arts and Science is now an Autonomous college having an objective of providing the most relevant education to its students through the most appropriate teaching-learning methods. The College is carrying out an experiment to studying the impact of its policies on study habits and study patterns of its students (enrolled under the autonomous structure) who, it believes, are responsible adults and have taken admission to this College with an aim of doing well in life. In this context the College proposes to demarcate one Continuous Assessment as a mid-semester assessment (MSA). This MSA will be in the month of January/February (the exact dates will be notified by individual departments). A special privilege will be granted to students who have 75 percent attendance prior to answering the mid-semester assessment and score at least 50 percent of the total marks in the mid-semester assessment. The privilege that students, who fulfill both the conditions specified above, will be granted is that they will be exempted from the 75 percent attendance rule for the remaining part of the semester. They, however, will have to fulfill all other conditions pertaining to their study which includes answering the remaining Continuous Assessments as and when they are held. The exemption under this experiment is granted only to students of semester two (First Year) and semester four (Second Year), pursuing B.A. or B.Sc. degrees, who have been admitted to the autonomous programmes of the College. As usual, regular lectures will continue to be conducted with attendance taken in every course for the remaining part of the semester. The above privilege cannot be claimed by students who do not belong to the category specified above, i.e. having an attendance of at least 75 percent prior to answering the mid-semester exam and a score of at least 50 percent in the mid-semester exam.
